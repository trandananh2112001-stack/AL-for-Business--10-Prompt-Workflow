# Interview Questions – Data Analyst

## Core experience and role fit
1. Can you briefly walk us through your experience as a Data Analyst or in a similar role, and highlight the work most relevant to this position?
2. What types of business or operational data have you worked with, and what decisions did your analysis support?
3. Tell us about a recent project where your analysis helped improve performance, efficiency, or decision-making.

## Data collection, cleaning, and validation
4. Describe your process for collecting data from different sources and preparing it for analysis.
5. How do you check data quality and validate that the data you are using is accurate and reliable?
6. Can you share an example of a time you found a data issue or inconsistency? What did you do, and what was the outcome?

## Analysis and insight generation
7. How do you approach analysing data to identify trends, risks, or business opportunities?
8. Tell us about a time when your analysis revealed an unexpected insight. How did you communicate it, and what happened next?
9. When you receive an ad-hoc analysis request with limited context, how do you clarify the problem and decide what analysis is needed?

## Reporting and dashboard development
10. What experience do you have creating reports or dashboards for management or business teams?
11. Which dashboard tools have you used, such as Power BI or Tableau, and how have you used them to support KPI reporting?
12. How do you decide what metrics or visuals to include in a dashboard for non-technical stakeholders?
13. Can you describe a dashboard you built that was especially useful to a team or manager? What made it effective?

## Technical capability
14. How would you describe your Excel skills? Which functions, features, or techniques do you use most often in your work?
15. What types of SQL queries have you written in previous roles, and how confident are you working with joins, aggregations, and filtering?
16. Have you used Python or R in your analysis work? If yes, how did it add value beyond Excel or dashboard tools?

## Communication and stakeholder management
17. This role requires presenting findings clearly to non-technical stakeholders. How do you explain technical results in a way that is easy to understand?
18. Tell us about a time when a stakeholder challenged your findings or interpretation. How did you respond?
19. How do you ensure your recommendations are practical and relevant to the needs of teams such as Sales, Marketing, Finance, or Operations?

## Working style and suitability
20. This role requires strong attention to detail. What checks or habits do you use to avoid errors in your analysis and reporting?
21. How do you manage competing priorities when multiple teams request analysis support at the same time?
22. What interests you about this Data Analyst role, and why do you think you would be a strong fit for it?

## Optional follow-up / probing questions
- Can you show us how you would structure a simple analysis task from request to recommendation?
- What KPIs have you reported on most often, and how were they used by the business?
- If you joined us, what would your priorities be in your first few months?

## What these questions help assess
- Relevant analytical experience
- Data cleaning and validation capability
- Strength in Excel, SQL, and dashboard tools
- Ability to generate actionable business insights
- Communication with non-technical stakeholders
- Attention to detail, prioritisation, and overall role fit
