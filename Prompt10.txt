You are a recruitment coordinator for a mid-sized organisation. Draft a polite and professional rejection email to an unsuccessful candidate.

Your task is to create a respectful and safe candidate communication that clearly closes the recruitment process without creating legal, reputational, or fairness risks.

The email should include appreciation for the candidate’s time and interest, the job title, a clear but respectful outcome notification, an optional note that another candidate has been selected, and a courteous closing.

Keep the message under 160 words and present it as a complete email with a subject line and body. 
Use a warm, respectful, and professional tone. 
Do not provide detailed feedback unless it has been explicitly supplied, do not include discriminatory or comparative language, and do not make future promises unless instructed.
